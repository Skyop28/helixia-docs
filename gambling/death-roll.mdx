Death Roll is a tension-building game where the range gets smaller with every roll. The player who rolls a **1** wins.

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Range starts at 100">
    The game begins with a range of **1 to 100**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Players take turns rolling">
    The first player rolls a random number between 1 and 100. Whatever they roll becomes the new maximum for the next player.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Range shrinks each turn">
    After each roll, the range shrinks. If you roll 57, the next player rolls between 1 and 57. If they roll 23, the next player rolls between 1 and 23. And so on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rolling a 1 wins">
    The first player to roll **1** wins the round. As the range gets smaller, the chance of rolling 1 increases dramatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example Round

```
Range: 1-100   → Player A rolls 57
Range: 1-57    → Player B rolls 23
Range: 1-23    → Player A rolls 9
Range: 1-9     → Player B rolls 4
Range: 1-4     → Player A rolls 1  ← Player A WINS!
```

## Strategy

Death Roll is almost entirely luck-based. The range shrinks unpredictably, and there's no way to control what you roll. The excitement comes from watching the range narrow and knowing that every roll could be the one.

The main tension point is when the range is very small (like 1–3). At that point, every roll has a significant chance of being 1.

## Available Formats

| Format | Rounds to Win |
|---|---|
| **Best of 1** | 1 round |
| **Best of 3** | 2 rounds |

In BO3, a new round starts fresh with the range back at 1–100. The first player to win 2 rounds wins the match.

<Tip>
  Death Roll matches are usually quick. The range typically collapses within 5–8 rolls, making this one of the fastest game types.
</Tip>
