When starting a gamble, you choose a **format** that determines how many rounds are played. The format affects the match length and how much room there is for comebacks.

## Available Formats

### Best of 1 (BO1)

The simplest format. **One round decides everything.** Win the round, win the match.

- Fastest format
- No room for comebacks
- Available for all game types
- Best for quick gambles or when you want a decisive result

### Best of 3 (BO3)

**First to 2 round wins** takes the match. This means a minimum of 2 rounds and a maximum of 3.

- Balanced format
- Allows one comeback after a loss
- Available for High/Low, Death Roll, and Buckshot Roulette
- The most popular format for serious gambles

### Best of 5 (BO5)

**First to 3 round wins** takes the match. Minimum of 3 rounds, maximum of 5.

- Longest format
- Multiple comebacks possible
- **Only available for Pokémon Rand**
- Best when you want an extended match

## Format Availability by Game

| Game | BO1 | BO3 | BO5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| High/Low | ✅ | ✅ | — |
| Death Roll | ✅ | ✅ | — |
| Buckshot Roulette | ✅ | ✅ | — |
| Pokémon Rand | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |

## How Scoring Works

In multi-round formats, the score is tracked and displayed throughout the match. For example, in a BO3 match, you might see:

```
Score: 1 - 0
```

This means the first player has won 1 round and the second player has won 0. The first player needs 1 more round win to take the match, while the second player needs 2.

## Choosing a Format

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="When to pick BO1">
    Choose BO1 when you want a quick, decisive result. There's no second chance — one roll, one outcome. This is also good when the wager is very large and you don't want the tension of multiple rounds.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="When to pick BO3">
    BO3 is the sweet spot for most gambles. It's long enough to feel fair (bad luck in one round can be recovered) but short enough to stay exciting. Most experienced gamblers prefer BO3.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="When to pick BO5">
    BO5 is only for Pokémon Rand and provides the most rounds. Since individual Rand rounds are very quick (just stat comparisons), BO5 keeps the match interesting without dragging on.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
