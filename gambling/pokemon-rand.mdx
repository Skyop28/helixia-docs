Pokémon Rand is a unique gambling game that uses real Pokémon stats. Players roll random Pokémon and compare a chosen stat. Higher stat wins the round.

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="First player picks a stat">
    The first player (usually the initiator) rolls a number between **1 and 7** to select which stat will be compared:

    | Roll | Stat |
    |---|---|
    | 1 | HP |
    | 2 | Attack |
    | 3 | Defense |
    | 4 | Sp. Atk |
    | 5 | Sp. Def |
    | 6 | Speed |
    | 7 | Total (sum of all stats) |
  </Step>
  <Step title="First player rolls a Pokémon">
    A random number between **1 and 1025** is generated, corresponding to a Pokémon in the National Pokédex. That Pokémon's selected stat is revealed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Second player rolls a Pokémon">
    The second player also gets a random Pokémon. The same stat is compared.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Higher stat wins">
    The player whose Pokémon has the higher value for the chosen stat wins the round.

    If both Pokémon have the **same** stat value, the round is a tie — no points are awarded and the round is skipped.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example

> **Round 1:** Stat rolled is **Attack** (2)
>
> Player A gets **Garchomp** (Attack: 130)
> Player B gets **Pikachu** (Attack: 55)
>
> Player A wins the round!

## Strategy

Pokémon Rand is primarily luck-based — both the stat and the Pokémon are random. However, some stats are more volatile than others:

- **Total** (7) tends to produce closer matchups since it averages all stats
- **Speed** (6) has a wide range, creating more dramatic differences
- **HP** (1) tends to be higher overall but with big outliers

Since the stat is also randomly rolled, there's no way to control the outcome. It's a pure test of luck.

## Available Formats

| Format | Rounds to Win |
|---|---|
| **Best of 1** | 1 round |
| **Best of 3** | 2 rounds |
| **Best of 5** | 3 rounds |

Pokémon Rand is the **only game type** that supports Best of 5 format. This is because individual rounds are very fast (just stat comparisons), so longer matches keep things interesting.

<Tip>
  BO5 matches are great if you want a more extended experience. With 5 potential rounds, comebacks are possible and the match feels more skill-testing, even though each round is random.
</Tip>
