High/Low is the simplest gambling game. The initiator picks a strategy, both players roll, and the dice decide the winner.

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Initiator chooses HIGH or LOW">
    After the game starts, the initiator (the person who sent the challenge) chooses:
    - **HIGH** — the higher roll wins
    - **LOW** — the lower roll wins
  </Step>
  <Step title="Both players roll">
    The bot rolls a random number between **1 and 100** for each player. The rolls happen automatically — no action needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compare rolls">
    - If the initiator chose **HIGH**: the player with the higher roll wins the round.
    - If the initiator chose **LOW**: the player with the lower roll wins the round.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Ties

If both players roll the same number, the round is a **draw** and is re-rolled. No score changes — the round simply happens again with new rolls.

## Strategy

Choosing HIGH or LOW is purely a mind game — the dice are random regardless. There's no mathematical advantage to either choice. It's about reading your opponent and making a decision.

## Available Formats

| Format | Rounds to Win |
|---|---|
| **Best of 1** | 1 round |
| **Best of 3** | 2 rounds |

In a BO3 match, the first player to win 2 rounds wins the match and takes the pot.

## Example

> **Player A** challenges **Player B** for 100,000 PC in High/Low BO1.
>
> Player A chooses **HIGH**.
>
> Player A rolls **73**. Player B rolls **41**.
>
> Player A wins — their roll (73) was higher. Player A receives 200,000 PC (their 100k back + Player B's 100k).
